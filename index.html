How INR is Calculated

INR = (Patient PT ÷ Mean Normal PT)ISI

Input Definitions

  • Patient PT: Your prothrombin time in seconds from a blood test. Measures how long it takes your blood to clot.
  • Mean Normal PT: The average PT value from healthy individuals tested with the same reagent/instrument. Provided by your lab.
  • ISI (International Sensitivity Index): A calibration value for the thromboplastin reagent used in testing. Ranges from 0.8-3.0; lower values indicate more sensitive reagents.

Pro Tips

  • Get your blood tests at the same time of day for consistency
  • Keep a food diary if you notice INR fluctuations (vitamin K intake matters)
  • Always inform new healthcare providers that you take anticoagulants
  • Wear a medical alert bracelet if you're on long-term anticoagulation

Warning Signs: Call Your Doctor Immediately

If you experience any of these while taking warfarin (Coumadin), seek medical attention right away:

  • Unusual bleeding: Nosebleeds that won't stop, bleeding gums, coughing/vomiting blood
  • Blood in urine or stool: Pink, red, or brown urine; bright red or black/tarry stools
  • Severe headaches, dizziness, or weakness: Could indicate bleeding in the brain
  • Unexplained bruising: Large bruises or bruises that grow larger
  • Heavy menstrual bleeding: Soaking through pads/tampons much faster than usual
  • Cuts that won't stop bleeding: Even minor cuts that bleed for more than 10 minutes
  • Severe stomach pain: Could indicate internal bleeding
  • Signs of stroke: Sudden numbness, confusion, trouble speaking, vision changes, loss of balance

Also Notify Your Provider About:

  • Starting or stopping any medications (including OTC drugs and supplements)
  • Significant changes in diet, especially vitamin K-rich foods (leafy greens)
  • Illnesses like diarrhea, vomiting, or fever that last more than 24 hours
  • Planned surgeries, dental procedures, or new medical tests
  • Changes in alcohol consumption
  • Pregnancy or plans to become pregnant
  • Missed doses of your anticoagulant medication
